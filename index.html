How to Ask Better Underwriting Questions

Underwriting questions are rarely simple. The more specific context you provide about the layered details, the more accurate, practical, and actionable the guidance will be.

Key information to include (when relevant):
  • Core numbers: Credit score, DTI, LTV/CLTV, loan amount, reserves, property type/occupancy.
  • The specific issue: What the underwriter (or AUS) is flagging, or your exact concern.
  • Timeline & events: Dates for recent BK, foreclosure, late payments, job changes, etc.
  • Employment & income: Self-employed? How long? Any gaps or multiple jobs?
  • Compensating factors: What strengths does the borrower have (strong credit history, large down payment, low LTV, stable employment, etc.)?
  • What you've already considered: Any compensating factors or documentation you've pulled.
Pro move: Upload your documents

Drop your current Ruoff overlays, the specific investor guideline, or AUS findings. The analysis will reference them directly for much more precise results.

Example of a strong, detailed question:
"FHA purchase. Borrower 695 credit, Chapter 7 BK discharged 20 months ago. Gift funds from parents for full down payment + closing costs. DTI 49% with new payment. Self-employed 18 months (tax returns show income). Strong reserves otherwise. What compensating factors or additional documentation would most help approval? Any red flags with the gift of equity?"

After you receive an answer, use the "Continue this scenario" area below the results to ask follow-ups like "What if we add a co-borrower with 720 credit?" or "Generate a conditions checklist for the file."
